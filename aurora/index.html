Observation tips
Get away from city lights — light pollution is the biggest obstacle. Even 30km from a city makes a big difference.
Let your eyes dark-adapt for 20 minutes before judging what you can see.
Look north (in the northern hemisphere). Aurora typically starts as a faint green glow on the northern horizon.
Camera phones can often see aurora that eyes can't — use night mode with a long exposure.
Kp index updates every 15 minutes. Check around midnight local time for best chances.
Solar flares and CMEs can cause sudden storm onset with little warning — subscribe to NOAA space weather alerts.
