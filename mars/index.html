Active Mars Missions
Perseverance (NASA) — Jezero Crater, active since Feb 2021. Collecting samples, operating Ingenuity helicopter. 70+ helicopter flights.
Curiosity (NASA) — Gale Crater, active since Aug 2012. Climbing Mount Sharp. RTG-powered, no end date planned.
Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (NASA) — Orbiting since 2006. High-res imaging, weather data. 500TB+ data returned.
MAVEN (NASA) — Studying atmospheric escape. Key for understanding how Mars lost its atmosphere.
Mars Express (ESA) — Orbiting since 2003. Radar confirmed subsurface liquid water in 2018.
Tianwen-1 / Zhurong (CNSA) — Orbiter active. Zhurong rover went silent May 2022.
Mars Weather Facts
Mars dust storms can grow to cover the entire planet. They last months and reduce solar power to rovers by 90%.
Temperatures can swing 100°C between day and night at the equator.
The thin atmosphere provides little insulation — bare skin would instantly freeze then boil (pressure too low for liquid water).
Dust devils are common and can reach 8 km high — taller than any storm on Earth.
Mars has seasons like Earth, but they last nearly twice as long due to its 687-day year.
MOXIE aboard Perseverance has successfully produced oxygen from CO₂ — a key technology for future human missions.
