About Reentry Risk
Most objects burn up. Atmospheric friction creates extreme heat — aluminium melts at 660°C, and most satellites are made of aluminium or composites that vaporise completely.
Dense materials survive. Titanium, stainless steel, and iron can survive reentry. Rocket fuel tanks and reaction wheels are the most common survivors.
The risk to individuals is tiny. You are roughly 65,000× more likely to be struck by lightning than by falling space debris in your lifetime.
Uncontrolled reentries are bad practice. Most space agencies plan controlled deorbits to target the South Pacific "spacecraft cemetery" — the most remote ocean area on Earth.
China's Long March 5B has drawn international criticism for routinely making uncontrolled reentries of its large (22-tonne) core stages.
