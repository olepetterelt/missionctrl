Flight 1 — IFT-1
Apr 20, 2023 · Boca Chica, TX
Partial success
First integrated launch attempt
Both stages ignited and the vehicle cleared the pad. Multiple Raptor engines failed during ascent. Vehicle began tumbling ~4 min after launch. Flight termination system activated. Pad infrastructure severely damaged (no flame deflector).

Jan 16, 2025 · Starbase, TX
Partial success
Booster caught again ✓ · Ship anomaly during reentry
First Block 2 ship (S33). Booster successfully caught for the third time. Ship experienced an energetic event in the aft section caused by propellant leaks from harmonic response stronger than expected. Debris fell over Turks & Caicos — FAA opened investigation.

Booster caught ✓ · Ship engine failure during reentry
Hardware failure in one of Ship's center Raptor engines caused inadvertent propellant mixing and ignition. Ship disintegrated over the Bahamas and West Indies. Booster was caught successfully. FAA expanded hazard area for next flight.

Why Starship matters
Starship is designed to be fully and rapidly reusable — both the booster and the ship. If achieved, it would reduce the cost of getting to orbit by 10-100× compared to current rockets.

It is NASA's only selected lunar lander for Artemis (HLS) and is SpaceX's intended vehicle for crewed Mars missions. The ability to refuel in orbit allows it to carry much heavier payloads beyond Earth orbit than any rocket in history.

Elon Musk has stated a target cost of under $10M per launch at scale — compared to ~$4B for SLS and ~$70M for Falcon 9.
