Magnitude guide
Mag <0 — rivals Venus in brightness
Mag 0–3 — naked eye from most locations
Mag 3–6 — naked eye from dark sites
Mag 6–8 — binoculars needed
Mag 8–12 — small telescope (100–200mm)
Mag >12 — large telescope required
How to observe
Use binoculars — most comets look like fuzzy stars, not movie comets
Observe from a dark site, away from city lights
Allow 20 min for eyes to dark-adapt before observing
The comet's tail always points away from the Sun
Track it over several nights — movement confirms it's a comet
Discovery programs
ATLAS — Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System. Major comet/asteroid survey.
Pan-STARRS — Panoramic Survey Telescope. Discovers dozens of comets yearly.
ZTF — Zwicky Transient Facility. Wide-field survey.
Rubin/LSST — Will dramatically increase discovery rates from ~2025.
